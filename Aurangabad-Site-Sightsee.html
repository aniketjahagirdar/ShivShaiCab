Aurangabad Site Sightsee

1)Ellora Cavas

  • The Aurangabad Caves, also known as the Ellora Caves, are a group of 34 Buddhist, Hindu and Jain caves located in Aurangabad, Maharashtra, India.
  • They are considered to be one of the finest examples of rock-cut architecture in India.
  • The caves were built over a period of 600 years, from the 6th to the 11th centuries.
  • The caves are divided into three groups: the Buddhist caves, the Hindu caves, and the Jain caves.
  • The Buddhist caves are the oldest, and they date back to the 6th and 7th centuries.
  • The Hindu caves were built in the 7th and 8th centuries, and they are more elaborate and ornate than the Buddhist caves.
  • The Jain caves were built in the 9th and 10th centuries, and they are the most decorated of the three groups.

  • The Aurangabad Caves are a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and they are one of the most popular tourist destinations in India.

    • 2)Sleeping Hanuman Mandir

  • Bhadra Maruti is a Hindu temple dedicated to the god Hanuman, located in Aurangabad, Maharashtra, India.
  • The temple is believed to have been built in the 12th century, and it is one of the most important Hanuman temples in India.
  • The temple is located on a hilltop, and it offers stunning views of the city of Aurangabad.
  • The temple is dedicated to Hanuman, the monkey god, who is considered to be a symbol of strength and devotion.
  • The temple is decorated with intricate carvings of Hanuman and other Hindu deities.
  • The temple is open to visitors from 6:00 AM to 8:00 PM, and the entrance fee is INR 5 for adults and INR 2 for children.
  • Visitors are advised to wear comfortable shoes and clothing, and to bring a hat and sunscreen.
  • It is important to be respectful of the Hindu faith and its practices when visiting the temple.

    • 3)Daulatabad Fort/ Devgiri Fort

  • Deogiri port is a historic port located in the Aurangabad district of Maharashtra, India.
  • The port was built by the Yadava dynasty in the 12th century and was used to trade with the Middle East and Southeast Asia.
  • The port declined in importance after the Mughal conquest of the Deccan in the 16th century.
  • The ruins of the port can still be seen today and are a popular tourist destination.
  • The port was located on the banks of the Godavari River and was an important trading center for the Yadava dynasty.
  • The port declined in importance after the Mughal conquest of the Deccan due to the silting of the Godavari River and the rise of the port of Surat.
  • The ruins of the port can still be seen today and are a popular tourist destination.

    • 4)Bibi-ka-Maqbara

  • Location: Bibi Ka Maqbara is located in Aurangabad, Maharashtra, India.
  • History: It was built by Mughal emperor Aurangzeb in memory of his wife Rabia-ud-Durrani.
  • Design: The mausoleum is an imitation of the Taj Mahal in Agra, but it is smaller and less elaborate.
  • Opening hours: The mausoleum is open from 6:00 AM to 8:00 PM, all year round.
  • Ticket price: The ticket price for adults is INR 25, and the ticket price for children is INR 10.
  • How to get there: Bibi Ka Maqbara can be reached by road, rail, or air. The nearest airport is Aurangabad Airport, which is about 10 km away.

    • 5)Pitalkhora Caves

  • The Pitalkhora Caves are a group of 14 rock-cut caves located in the Satmala range of the Western Ghats in Maharashtra, India.
  • The caves are dated to the 2nd century BCE to 3rd century CE.
  • The caves are dedicated to the Hinayana school of Buddhism.
  • The caves are carved out of a basalt rock hillock and consist of a variety of chambers, including chaityas and viharas.
  • The caves are decorated with intricate carvings of Buddhist deities, scenes from the life of the Buddha, and other religious symbols.
  • The caves are a popular tourist destination and are located about 40 kilometers from Aurangabad city.
  • Some of the important features of the Pitalkhora Caves include:
  • The Chaitya Hall (Cave 3) is the largest and most elaborate cave in the complex.
  • The Vihara (Cave 1) is a smaller cave that was used as a monastery.
  • The Waterfall Cave (Cave 4) is located next to a waterfall.

    • 6)Sonwari Mahal

  • Sonwari Mahal is a historical palace located in Aurangabad city, Maharashtra, India.
  • It is situated in the campus of Babasaheb Ambedkar Marathwada University.
  • The palace was built in 1650 by Sambhaji Maharaj, the sixth son of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj.
  • It was used as a residence by Sambhaji Raje.
  • Sonwari Mahal is a beautiful and grand palace.
  • It is built in red stone and its walls are decorated with gold plating.
  • The interior of the palace has many beautiful arches, pillars, and sculptures.
  • Sonwari Mahal is a popular tourist destination.
  • Thousands of tourists visit it every year.

    • 7)Panchaki

  • Panchaki is a Hindu festival celebrated in the month of Chaitra (March-April) in Maharashtra, India.
  • The festival is dedicated to the five elements of nature: earth, water, fire, air, and space.
  • The festival is celebrated over five days, starting with Gudi Padwa, the first day of the Chaitra month.
  • On each day, a different element is worshipped.
  • The festival is a time for family and friends to come together and celebrate the beginning of the new year.
  • It is also a time to pray for good health, prosperity, and happiness.

  • These include portraits of the Maharaja, weapons and objects he used, and artifacts related to the history and culture of the Maratha Empire.
  • The museum is a popular tourist destination and is visited by thousands of tourists every year.
  • The museum has a large inscription at the entrance with a beautiful sculpture of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj.
  • The museum has many halls with various artifacts and objects on display.
  • One hall has artifacts related to the life and work of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj.
